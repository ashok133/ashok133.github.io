Financial Distress Prediction - Determining a firm's likelihood of defaulting based on historical financial information




June 2018 - June 2018

  • Problem Statement

    To assess the usefulness of financial statement data in assessing the firm's credit-worthiness and predict the likelihood that a firm will default on its debt obligations or file for bankruptcy.

    ​
  • Description

    Financial information is presumed to be useful for external users like regulators, investors, and creditors. Given numerical data on various entities in the balance sheet, income statement and cash flow for 50 firms that defaulted in the following year, the objective was to create financial ratios that can differentiate default and non-default firms. The three stages followed were - verification of data, investigation of plausible ratios to predict distress, and test the developed model on a sample of unknown firms. A linear regression model was created with a credible R-square achieving a PCC (Probability of Correct Classification) of 71% (accuracy measure) on a default decision threshold equal to 0.4 on test samples.

    Financial ratios identified:

    1. CR (Current Ratio)
    2. CFOS (Cash Flow from Operations to Sales)
    3. Debt to Equity ratio
    4. Sales Ratio
